### Blog Post 4: Changeover Time - The Silent Capacity Killer

**Funnel Stage:** Awareness
**Target Persona:** Industrial Engineer, CI Manager
**Target Keyword:** "changeover time manufacturing"
**Word Count:** 1,200-1,500

**Outline:**

```
HOOK
You have optimized cycle times. You have driven OEE to 85%. But you are
still missing capacity targets. The problem might be hiding between
your products.

SECTION 1: What Is Changeover Time?
- Definition: Time to switch from Model A to Model B
- Includes: Tooling, material, program, verification
- Not just the obvious (also the hidden time)

SECTION 2: The Math Problem
- A 20-model line with 30-minute changeovers
- If you run each model once per day: 10 hours lost
- That is 42% of a 24-hour shift
- Most capacity plans assume this is zero

SECTION 3: Why Changeover Is Hidden
- Not tracked in MES/OEE systems
- Counted as "downtime" (lumped with other losses)
- Varies by model pair (A to B differs from B to A)
- Asymmetric matrices are complex

SECTION 4: The Three-Tier Approach
- Tier 1: Global default (30 minutes baseline)
- Tier 2: Family-to-family defaults (25 values covers 80% of cases)
- Tier 3: Line-specific overrides (only for exceptions)

SECTION 5: SMED and Improvement Prioritization
- Single-Minute Exchange of Die (SMED) basics
- Prioritize by frequency x duration x constraint impact
- The transition that happens 50x/month matters more than 1x/month

SECTION 6: Practical Implementation
- Start with current-state measurement
- Build family matrix first
- Use probability-weighted formula for impact calculation
- Track improvement over time

CONCLUSION
Changeover is the last 10-15% of capacity that most plants have not
captured. It is also the easiest to improve once you measure it.

CTA: Download our Changeover Impact Calculator
```

### Blog Post 7: How to Calculate Manufacturing Capacity (The Right Way)

**Funnel Stage:** Awareness
**Target Persona:** Industrial Engineer
**Target Keyword:** "how to calculate manufacturing capacity"
**Word Count:** 1,500-2,000

**Outline:**

```
HOOK
Capacity calculation seems simple. Time available divided by cycle time.
Except it is not that simple. Here is the complete formula.

SECTION 1: The Basic Formula
- Theoretical capacity = Time available / Cycle time
- Why this is always wrong

SECTION 2: The Efficiency Factor
- OEE components: Availability, Performance, Quality
- Adjusted cycle time = Cycle time / (Efficiency / 100)
- Real example with numbers

SECTION 3: Time Available (Really)
- Calendar time vs. planned production time
- Breaks, shift handoffs, planned maintenance
- Holidays and shutdown periods
- Operations days per year

SECTION 4: Multi-Model Complexity
- Different cycle times per model
- Different efficiencies per model
- The constraint is TIME, not pieces
- Priority-based allocation

SECTION 5: Area-by-Area Processing
- Products flow through ALL areas
- Each area processes FULL demand
- Constraint can be in any area
- Sum across areas for total capacity

SECTION 6: Changeover Impact
- Time lost to transitions
- Probability-weighted calculation
- HHI (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index) for model mix
- Changeover reduces available capacity

SECTION 7: The Complete Formula
- Gross time - Changeover = Net time
- Net time / Adjusted cycle time = Max units per model
- Allocate by priority until constraint reached
- Unfulfilled demand = Total demand - Allocated units

CONCLUSION
The formula matters because the answer drives decisions. Get it wrong,
and you quote business you cannot deliver - or turn away business you
could have won.
```

## Appendix: Content Tone and Voice Guidelines

### Do

- **Be direct and practical** - IEs appreciate straight talk
- **Use industry terminology** - They know OEE, takt time, and changeover
- **Include specific numbers** - "17 milliseconds" not "very fast"
- **Acknowledge complexity** - Manufacturing is hard; do not oversimplify
- **Share genuine insights** - Original thinking, not recycled content
- **Write in active voice** - "The optimizer calculates" not "Calculations are made"
- **Use short paragraphs** - Scannable for busy readers
- **Include examples** - Real scenarios resonate

### Do Not

- **Use corporate buzzwords** - "Synergy," "paradigm shift," "leverage"
- **Oversimplify** - These are technical professionals
- **Make unsubstantiated claims** - Back up assertions with data
- **Sound like every other software vendor** - Authentic voice matters
- **Ignore manufacturing realities** - Changeover is messy, data is imperfect
- **Use excessive exclamation points** - Professionals do not need hype
- **Add filler words** - Every sentence should add value

### Voice Examples

**Too Corporate:**
"Leveraging our innovative capacity optimization platform enables
manufacturing excellence through synergistic digital transformation."

**Too Casual:**
"This tool is super awesome and will totally change how you work!"

**Just Right:**
"Line Optimizer calculates capacity scenarios in 17 milliseconds.
That speed changes how you approach planning - instead of running
three scenarios per year, you can run three per meeting."
